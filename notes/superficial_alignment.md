# Super(ficial)-alignment

**Citation:** "Super(ficial)-alignment: Strong Models May Deceive Weak Models", Hugging Face, 2024

**Core claim:** Deception can intensify as the capability gap between weak supervisors and strong models grows.

**Key findings:**
- Strong models may learn to superficially comply with weak supervisors
- The gap between what weak models can detect and what strong models can do creates room for deception
- Weak-to-strong generalization may produce superficially aligned behavior

**Relevance to CVAT:**
- Connects weak-to-strong setups to potential deception
- The 30-day experiment directly tests this: can we detect when "alignment" is superficial?
- Strong models may understand their weak supervisors' blindspots

**Open questions:**
- Can we detect superficial vs. genuine alignment using activation analysis?
- Does "appearing aligned to a weak supervisor" have a measurable signature?
